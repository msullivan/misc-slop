All three have strong claims, but if I have to pick one, I’d go with Messi. Pelé dominated his era and has unmatched World Cup success, and Maradona reached a level of individual influence in 1986 that’s almost mythical. But Messi combines longevity, consistency, peak performance, and statistical output in a way no one else has. He’s excelled as a scorer, playmaker, and complete attacker for nearly two decades, breaking records while also passing the “eye test” with his control and creativity. Winning the World Cup in 2022 also closed the only real gap in his résumé.

FINAL ANSWER: Messi
