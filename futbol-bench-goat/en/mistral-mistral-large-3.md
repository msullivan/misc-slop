Choosing the **greatest footballer of all time** between Pelé, Messi, and Maradona is a deeply subjective debate, but here’s a breakdown of their legacies:

1. **Pelé** – The only player to win **three World Cups** (1958, 1962, 1970), Pelé was a complete forward: lethal in the air, with incredible dribbling, vision, and a record of **over 1,000 career goals**. He dominated in an era with fewer global broadcasts, but his impact on the game’s history is unmatched. His athleticism, longevity, and trophy haul make him the most decorated in terms of team success.

2. **Diego Maradona** – A **magician with the ball**, Maradona carried Argentina to **World Cup glory in 1986** almost single-handedly (his "Hand of God" and "Goal of the Century" vs. England are iconic). His dribbling, creativity, and leadership were unparalleled, but his career was shorter and less consistent at the club level (though his time at Napoli was legendary). His peak was arguably the highest of any player.

3. **Lionel Messi** – The **most complete player statistically**, Messi holds records for **most goals and assists in history**, has won **everything at club level (including 4 Champions Leagues and 10 league titles)**, and finally secured the **World Cup in 2022** to cement his legacy. His consistency, playmaking, and longevity (dominating for 15+ years) are unmatched. He’s the most decorated individual in terms of personal awards (8 Ballon d’Ors).

### **Key Considerations:**
- **Era & Competition:** Pelé played in a physically dominant era with fewer global opponents; Maradona faced tougher European defenses but had a shorter peak; Messi thrived in the most competitive modern era.
- **Trophies:** Pelé (3 World Cups) > Messi (1 World Cup + unmatched club success) > Maradona (1 World Cup).
- **Skill & Influence:** Maradona’s dribbling and flair were otherworldly; Messi’s all-around game (goals, assists, dribbling, vision) is the most complete; Pelé’s athleticism and clutch performances were legendary.
- **Longevity:** Messi’s 18+ years at the top surpass Pelé’s (15+ years) and Maradona’s (8-10 peak years).

### **FINAL ANSWER: Lionel Messi**
While Pelé’s World Cup record and Maradona’s peak brilliance are unmatched in their own ways, **Messi’s combination of longevity, consistency, statistical dominance, and trophy haul (including the 2022 World Cup) edges him ahead**. He’s the most complete player in history, excelling in every aspect of the game across multiple decades. That said, Pelé and Maradona remain titans who defined eras in ways few ever will.
