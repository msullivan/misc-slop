While all three are undisputed legends who dominated different eras of the sport, **Lionel Messi** currently holds the strongest claim to the title of greatest of all time.

The case for Messi rests on an unparalleled combination of longevity, consistency, and completeness. He has maintained an elite level for nearly two decades, winning a record eight Ballon d'Or awards while breaking countless records for goals and assists. Unlike previous eras where players peaked for shorter windows, Messi has been consistently world-class from his teenage years into his mid-30s, adapting his game from a dribbling wizard to a deep-lying playmaker without losing effectiveness.

Crucially, Messi's 2022 World Cup victory with Argentina silenced the final major criticism of his career—that he hadn't won football's biggest prize for his country. His performance in that tournament, particularly in the final, mirrored Maradona's 1986 heroics but came against modern, athletically superior opposition and tactical complexity that earlier generations didn't face. He has also succeeded in multiple leagues (La Liga, Ligue 1, and MLS) and won the Copa América, demonstrating adaptability beyond Barcelona's system.

Pelé's case rests on three World Cups and goal-scoring records, though his era featured less global competition, athletic training, and tactical sophistication. Maradona possessed perhaps the most sublime individual skill and carried Argentina and Napoli to glory almost single-handedly, but his peak was shorter and punctuated by controversies that limited his total career output.

Messi combines Maradona's artistic genius with Pelé's statistical dominance and longevity, succeeding in the most competitive, analytically advanced era football has ever known.

FINAL ANSWER: Messi
