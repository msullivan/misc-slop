Choosing the greatest footballer of all time between Pelé, Lionel Messi, and Diego Maradona is an incredibly tough task, as each of these legends has left an indelible mark on the sport. Let’s break it down by considering their achievements, impact, and style of play.

**Pelé**: Often regarded as the original superstar of football, Pelé won three World Cups with Brazil (1958, 1962, and 1970), a feat unmatched by any other player. His goal-scoring record is staggering, with over 1,000 career goals (though some are debated due to friendlies). He played in an era with fewer protections for attackers, showcasing incredible skill, athleticism, and vision on often rough pitches. Pelé's global impact on popularizing football, especially in the Americas, is immense, and his charisma made him a cultural icon. However, critics argue he never tested himself in European club football, spending most of his career at Santos in Brazil.

**Lionel Messi**: Messi is often hailed as the most complete player of the modern era. His trophy cabinet is overflowing, with multiple La Liga titles, four Champions League titles, and a record seven Ballon d’Or awards. His dribbling, vision, playmaking, and goal-scoring (over 800 career goals) are unparalleled, often making the impossible look effortless. Messi finally won the World Cup in 2022 with Argentina, silencing critics who pointed to his lack of international success earlier in his career. Playing in the highly competitive European leagues (primarily with Barcelona), he has consistently performed against the best. Some argue, though, that his physicality and defensive work rate don’t match the all-around demands of past eras.

**Diego Maradona**: Maradona is celebrated for his raw passion, flair, and ability to single-handedly change games. His 1986 World Cup performance, leading Argentina to victory with iconic moments like the "Hand of God" and the "Goal of the Century" against England, is legendary. He also transformed Napoli, a struggling Italian club, into a powerhouse, winning two Serie A titles in an era when Italian football was brutally competitive. Maradona’s dribbling and creativity were mesmerizing, but his career was marred by personal struggles, inconsistency, and a shorter peak compared to Messi or Pelé. His impact, however, on and off the field, remains deeply emotional for fans worldwide.

**Explanation**: If we evaluate based on achievements, Messi stands out with his longevity, consistency, and sheer volume of individual and team honors, including the World Cup. Pelé’s World Cup record and pioneering role in football’s history are monumental, but his lack of European exposure and the different context of his era make direct comparisons tricky. Maradona’s peak was arguably the most spectacular, but his career lacks the sustained excellence of the other two. Messi’s ability to dominate in the modern, highly tactical, and physically demanding game, combined with his adaptability (from a winger to a playmaker), gives him a slight edge. He has also broken almost every record imaginable while maintaining a relatively controversy-free career.

**FINAL ANSWER: Lionel Messi**
