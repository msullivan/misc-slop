This is one of football's most debated questions. Here's my reasoning:

**Pelé** won three World Cups (1958, 1962, 1970), scored over 1,000 career goals, and dominated an era globally. However, the quality of Brazilian league football makes direct comparison harder.

**Maradona** delivered perhaps the single greatest individual World Cup performance in 1986, carrying a relatively average Argentina team to glory. His genius was undeniable, but career longevity and consistency were limited by injuries and personal struggles.

**Messi** has the strongest statistical and sustained excellence argument - multiple Ballon d'Or awards (8), Champions League titles, dominant La Liga records, and critically, he **finally won the World Cup in 2022**, which silenced a major criticism. He maintained elite performance across nearly two decades at the highest level of club football against the best opponents in the world, which is measurable in ways earlier eras weren't.

The strongest case arguably belongs to Messi because:
- Longevity at the elite level
- Statistics against the highest quality opponents
- Both club AND international success ultimately achieved
- Consistent greatness rather than peak brilliance

FINAL ANSWER: **Lionel Messi**

Though reasonable people can absolutely defend any of the three, and the debate itself reflects how extraordinary all of them were.
