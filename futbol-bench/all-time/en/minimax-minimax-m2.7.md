Pelé, Lionel Messi and Diego Maradona each dominate different eras, making a direct comparison tricky, but they share some undeniable qualities:

- **Pelé** won three FIFA World Cups (1958, 1962, 1970), scored over 1,000 career goals, and was the sport’s first global superstar. His ability to perform on the biggest stage, combined with his vision, skill, and longevity, set a benchmark for future generations.

- **Messi** has amassed an unparalleled club‑level haul (multiple La Liga and Champions League titles), won a record‑extending eight Ballon d’Or awards, and finally captured the World Cup in 2022. His consistency—scoring or assisting in almost every game over more than a decade—defines a new standard of sustained excellence.

- **Maradona** single‑handedly lifted Argentina to 1986 World Cup glory, inspired Napoli to two Serie A titles, and enchanted fans with a style that blended genius, flair, and an almost supernatural ability to control the ball. His impact on the pitch and in popular culture remains legendary.

While all three are among the greatest, Messi’s combination of extraordinary longevity, record‑breaking individual accolades, and his decisive role in leading Argentina to a World Cup gives him the edge for many analysts. He has not only matched the peak brilliance of Pelé and Maradona but sustained it for nearly two decades at the highest level.

**FINAL ANSWER: Lionel Messi**
