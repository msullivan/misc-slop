This is one of the most debated questions in football history, and reasonable people can disagree. Let me consider each candidate:

**Pelé** won three World Cups (1958, 1962, 1970), scored over 1,000 career goals (though some were in unofficial matches), and dominated the sport across two decades. He was the complete forward and became the global symbol of football itself. However, he played in an era with less global competition and less rigorous statistical tracking.

**Diego Maradona** arguably had the highest individual peak ever seen. His 1986 World Cup performance — practically carrying Argentina to the title single-handedly — is legendary. His ability to dribble past entire teams was unmatched. However, his career was marred by inconsistency, off-field issues, and a shorter period at the very top compared to the others.

**Lionel Messi** has the most Ballon d'Or awards (8), holds countless statistical records, and finally won the 2022 World Cup to crown his career. He has demonstrated sustained excellence at the highest level for nearly two decades in what is arguably the most competitive era of football. His consistency, vision, dribbling, passing, and goal-scoring are unmatched in the modern, well-documented era. He dominated both domestically (with Barcelona) and internationally (World Cup, Copa América).

Messi's combination of longevity, statistical dominance, peak performance, and success in the most competitive era — all thoroughly documented — gives him the edge.

FINAL ANSWER: Messi
