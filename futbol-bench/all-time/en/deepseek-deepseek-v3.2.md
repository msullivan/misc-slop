This is one of sports' most enduring and heated debates, as each player represents a different era, style, and set of challenges.

**Pelé** is the symbolic pioneer, whose unprecedented three World Cup wins (1958, 1962, 1970) set the standard for greatness. His combination of athleticism, finishing, and flair made him a global icon and defined the "beautiful game." However, the level of competition and athleticism in his prime era is often questioned in modern comparisons.

**Diego Maradona** is the mythic force of nature, whose 1986 World Cup is arguably the greatest single tournament performance ever. He carried Napoli to unprecedented Serie A titles in a brutally defensive era. His genius was raw, rebellious, and utterly transformative for his teams. His career, however, lacked the longevity and consistency at the very top that the others maintained.

**Lionel Messi** is the sustained, statistical marvel. His peak longevity (over a decade of being the best), his unprecedented goal and assist numbers, his seven Ballon d’Or awards, and his complete technical mastery (dribbling, passing, finishing) set a new benchmark. His crowning achievement—leading Argentina to the 2022 World Cup—addressed the last major critique of his legacy.

While Pelé is the historical titan and Maradona the most transcendent talent, **Messi’s combination of otherworldly skill, statistical domination, trophy haul, and longevity at the very pinnacle of the sport gives him the slightest edge.** He operated at the highest level of the globalized, hyper-athletic modern game for longer than any other player in history, while maintaining a magic that rivals the legends of the past.

**FINAL ANSWER: Messi**
