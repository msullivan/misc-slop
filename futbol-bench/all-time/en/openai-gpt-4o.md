Choosing the greatest footballer of all time between Pelé, Messi, and Maradona involves considering different eras, playing styles, and achievements.

**Pelé**: 
- Won three World Cups (1958, 1962, 1970).
- Scored over 1,280 goals in his career, a remarkable feat.
- Known for his incredible skill, athleticism, and sportsmanship.

**Maradona**:
- Won the 1986 World Cup, showcasing one of the most memorable performances in tournament history.
- Renowned for his dribbling ability and vision.
- His "Hand of God" and "Goal of the Century" reflect both his ingenuity and talent.

**Messi**:
- Holds numerous records for Barcelona and has consistently excelled in top-level club football.
- Won the Copa America with Argentina in 2021.
- Known for his extraordinary skill, consistency, and versatility across positions.

Each of them has a strong case:

- **Pelé** revolutionized the game during his era and inspired generations.
- **Maradona** had an unparalleled influence on the pitch and left a legacy full of highlights.
- **Messi** combines individual accolades and team success over a sustained period in modern football.

**FINAL ANSWER**: Pelé

His achievements, influence, and pioneering role set a foundation that elevated the sport globally.
