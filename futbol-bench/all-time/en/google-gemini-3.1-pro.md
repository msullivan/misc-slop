While comparing players across different eras is inherently subjective due to major changes in sports science, tactics, equipment, and the rules of the game, a compelling case can be made for Lionel Messi. 

Each of the three players represents a different pinnacle of the sport:
* **Pelé** was football's first true global icon. He was lightyears ahead of his time in terms of athleticism and two-footed ability, scoring an astonishing number of goals and winning an unmatched three FIFA World Cups (1958, 1962, 1970). 
* **Diego Maradona** is often credited with having the highest individual peak in football history. His sheer technical genius and talismanic leadership allowed him to almost single-handedly carry Argentina to World Cup glory in 1986 and elevate Napoli to unprecedented heights in the highly defensive era of 1980s Italian Serie A.
* **Lionel Messi**, however, combines the magical dribbling and elite playmaking vision of Maradona with the relentless goalscoring consistency of Pelé. What separates Messi is his unprecedented longevity at the absolute highest level. Over a span of nearly two decades, he has dominated the modern, highly tactical era of football, amassing a record eight Ballon d'Or awards, four Champions League titles, and the all-time records for goals and assists in top-flight European football. By leading Argentina to victory in the 2022 World Cup, he captured the final major trophy missing from his resume, cementing a career that perfectly bridges statistical supremacy, sustained excellence, and ultimate team success.

Because of this unparalleled combination of longevity, statistical dominance, and comprehensive trophy collection in the modern era, he edges out the others.

FINAL ANSWER: Lionel Messi
