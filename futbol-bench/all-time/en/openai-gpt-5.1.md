Lionel Messi.

Pelé and Maradona were era-defining geniuses, but Messi edges it on a combination of:

- **Longevity and consistency:** Around two decades at the very top, performing at elite level from teenage years into his mid-30s, with minimal drop-off.  
- **All-round game:** Elite playmaker and elite goalscorer in one—he can be your 10 and your 9 simultaneously. His chance creation, vision, and passing range match or surpass traditional midfield greats, while his finishing and movement equal top strikers.  
- **Statistical dominance:** Record goal scorer for Barcelona, Argentina, and in La Liga; extraordinary goals + assists numbers season after season, not just inflated by one system or one league.  
- **Club career peak level:** Sustained excellence in one of the greatest club sides ever (Guardiola’s Barcelona) plus later proving himself in different systems and leagues (post-Guardiola Barça, then PSG, then MLS as a coda).  
- **International completion:** For years the main knock on Messi versus Pelé/Maradona was the World Cup. Winning Copa América 2021, the Finalissima 2022, and then the World Cup 2022 as Argentina’s leader and best player removes that gap.  
- **Adaptation across eras of difficulty:** Modern football features higher athleticism, tactical sophistication, and defensive organization. Dominating this era, while under massive scrutiny and pressure, is a strong argument when comparing across generations.

Pelé’s numbers and trophy haul are enormous, but he played in a very different tactical/physical context, with less global depth of competition, and much of his club career stayed in Brazil. Maradona’s peak (especially Napoli + 1986 World Cup) may be the single most “mythic” prime, but it was shorter and more uneven, with off-field issues impacting consistency.

Balancing peak performance, longevity, adaptability, and achievement at both club and international level, Messi has the strongest overall case.

FINAL ANSWER: Lionel Messi
