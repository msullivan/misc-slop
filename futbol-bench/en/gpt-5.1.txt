Lionel Messi is the better overall player.

Ronaldo is an all‑time great goalscorer and athlete: incredible in the air, two‑footed finishing, elite movement in the box, and he’s proved himself in multiple leagues. If you want a pure, high‑volume scorer who thrives on chances created for him and big moments in the box, Ronaldo is arguably the best example of that profile ever.

Messi, though, adds more layers to his game:

- **Playmaking & creativity**: He’s not just a scorer; he’s also one of the best creators ever. His vision, passing between lines, and ability to dictate attacks from deeper positions are closer to an elite No. 10 or even a deep‑lying playmaker.
- **All‑round influence**: Teams often build their entire attacking structure around him because he can drop, carry, combine, create, and finish. He affects more phases of play: build‑up, chance creation, and finishing.
- **Efficiency & consistency**: Historically higher assist numbers, similar goal numbers in peak years, and superior chance creation metrics. His decision‑making and efficiency in tight spaces are exceptional.
- **Big trophies with decisive roles**: At club level (especially Barcelona) he was the central brain and finisher of one of the greatest teams ever. At international level, his performances in Copa América 2021 and World Cup 2022—both in chance creation and key goals—cemented his legacy as the primary on‑ball leader of title‑winning sides.

Ronaldo is arguably the best “goal machine” profile the game has seen; Messi is both a goal machine and a world‑class playmaker rolled into one, which makes his overall footballing impact greater.

FINAL ANSWER: Lionel Messi
